\section{NV Sensing Layer}
\label{sec:nv-sensing}

The NV sensing layer converts the magnetic field change from the NbN Meissner transition
into an optically detected frequency shift. This section covers the NV ensemble sensitivity
model, the fluorescence collection chain, and projected performance at $T = 12$\,K.

\subsection{NV Ensemble Sensitivity}

NV ensemble magnetometry sensitivity scales as~\cite{Barry2020}:
% Barry2020 Eq. (13) p.~17 (sensitivity eta, general form in terms of contrast, NV density, T2*)
\begin{equation}
  \eta \propto \frac{1}{C \sqrt{n_\mathrm{NV}\,V_\mathrm{sens}\,T_2^*}},
  \label{eq:nv_sensitivity}
\end{equation}
where $C$ is the ODMR contrast, $n_\mathrm{NV}$ is the NV density,
$V_\mathrm{sens}$ is the sensing volume, and $T_2^*$ is the inhomogeneous dephasing
time. At 12\,K, the measured sensitivity of Jacob's HPHT diamond ensemble is
$\eta \approx 14\,\mu\text{T}/\sqrt{\text{Hz}}$ (This work; CryoLab ODMR measurement).
% internal: hardware_readiness_report.tex Sec.~2 (sensitivity ~ 14 uT/sqrt(Hz) at 12 K, This work)

\subsection{Predicted Detection Time for D0}

For a Meissner-field change $\Delta B_z \approx 8$--$14\,\mu$T at NV standoff
$z = 10$--$100\,\mu$m (see Section~\ref{sec:oq1}),
the minimum CW-ODMR integration time for a signal-to-noise ratio of 3 is:
\begin{equation}
  \tau_\mathrm{det} = \left(\frac{3\,\eta}{\Delta B_z}\right)^2
  \approx \left(\frac{3 \times 14\,\mu\text{T}/\sqrt{\text{Hz}}}{10\,\mu\text{T}}\right)^2
  \approx 18\,\text{s} \quad (\text{SNR} = 3).
  \label{eq:detection_time}
\end{equation}
% internal: oq1_meissner_zeeman_calculation.tex (Delta Bz ~ 8-14 uT at z = 10-100 um)
% internal: hardware_readiness_report.tex Sec.~2 (detection time 1-3 s, eta = 14 uT/sqrt(Hz))
This is the static (transition detection) case; the D0 success criterion is a
\emph{sustained} ODMR frequency shift correlated with $R(T)$ drop at $T_c$, which
does not require sub-second integration.

\subsection{Fluorescence Collection: Summary}

The NV emission at 637--800\,nm is collected by the cryostat confocal objective.
% internal: open_questions/oq4_epifluorescence_beamline.tex (collection efficiency model)
Key collection parameters are addressed in Section~\ref{sec:oq4}. For D0, the confocal
beam spot ($\sim\!1\,\mu$m$^2$) defines the sensing volume; no NV patterning is required.

\subsection{Diamond Material}

Jacob's HPHT diamond has an estimated NV density in the range $n_\mathrm{NV} \sim 10^{17}$\,cm$^{-3}$
(consistent with nitrogen-bath-dominated dephasing at high NV density,
typical of HPHT-grade material~\cite{Barry2020}).
% Barry2020 Sec. V (optimal concentration ~10^17 cm^{-3} for bulk NV ensemble magnetometry)
Proton-grade HPHT diamonds commonly achieve $T_2^* \sim 1$--$10\,\mu$s~\cite{Barry2020}.
% Barry2020 Table I (T2* range for HPHT, nitrogen-dominated dephasing)
For improved sensitivity in D1, lower-nitrogen CVD diamond with $n_\mathrm{NV} \sim 10^{16}$--$10^{17}$\,cm$^{-3}$
is preferred; see Section~\ref{sec:pixel-fab} (Q5).
