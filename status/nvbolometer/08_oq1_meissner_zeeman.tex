\section{OQ-1: Meissner--Zeeman Calculation}
\label{sec:oq1}

Can we detect the ODMR Zeeman shift change when NbN transitions through $T_c$?
The answer (OQ-1 status: \textbf{RESOLVED}) is yes: the signal is detectable in
$\lesssim 1$\,s for NV--SC separation $z \leq 100\,\mu$m with a $B_\mathrm{bias} \sim 1$\,mT
applied field.
% internal: oq1_meissner_zeeman_calculation.tex Abstract

\subsection{Physical Setup}

The D0 demonstration places an NV diamond adjacent to an NbN thin film on sapphire.
A small permanent magnet provides bias field $B_\mathrm{bias}$ to lift the $\pm m_s$
degeneracy. When the sample cools through $T_c$, the NbN enters the Meissner state and
the local field at the NV changes by $\Delta B_z$.

\textbf{Key parameters:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item NV gyromagnetic ratio: $\gamma_{NV}/2\pi = 28.024$\,GHz/T~\cite{Doherty2013}.
    % Doherty2013 Table 1 p.~33 (NV Hamiltonian parameters, gamma_e = 28.024 GHz/T)
  \item Zero-field splitting at 12\,K: $D = 2877.5$\,MHz (This work; CryoLab).
  \item CW-ODMR sensitivity: $\eta = 14\,\mu\text{T}/\sqrt{\text{Hz}}$ (This work; CryoLab).
  \item NbN London penetration depth: $\lambda_L \approx 400$\,nm~\cite{Kamlapure2010}.
    % Kamlapure2010 (lambda_L for NbN films with T_c ~ 15.87 K; 400 nm within reported range for 50 nm film)
  \item NbN film thickness: $d_\mathrm{SC} = 200$\,nm (D0 design target).
  \item NbN $T_c \approx 15.7$\,K (This work; to be measured on deposited film).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Zeeman Shift Formula}

The NV ground-state ODMR frequencies~\cite{Barry2020,Doherty2013} are:
% Barry2020 Eq.~(6) (CW-ODMR transition frequencies); Doherty2013 p.~5 (NV Hamiltonian)
\begin{equation}
  f_\pm = D \pm \gamma_{NV}\,B_\mathrm{NV}.
  \label{eq:oq1_zeeman}
\end{equation}
A field change $\Delta B_z$ at the NV produces ODMR shift:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta\nu_\mathrm{signal} = 2\,\gamma_{NV}\,\Delta B_z.
  \label{eq:oq1_shift}
\end{equation}

Detection threshold for $\mathrm{SNR} = 1$ in time $\tau$:
$\Delta B_\mathrm{min}(\tau) = \eta/\sqrt{\tau} = 14\,\mu\text{T}/\sqrt{\tau[\text{Hz}^{-1}]}$.
In 1\,s: $\Delta B_\mathrm{min} = 14\,\mu$T (This work).

\subsection{Pearl Thin-Film Meissner Model}

For a thin SC film ($d_\mathrm{SC} < 2\lambda_L$), the Pearl length is~\cite{Pearl1964}:
% Pearl1964 p.65-66 (Pearl length Lambda = 2 lambda^2/d defined for thin-film vortex screening)
\begin{equation}
  \Lambda = \frac{2\lambda_L^2}{d_\mathrm{SC}}
  = \frac{2 \times (400\,\text{nm})^2}{200\,\text{nm}} = 1.6\,\mu\text{m}.
  \label{eq:oq1_pearl}
\end{equation}

The field change at NV height $z$ when the SC transitions (using the Pearl--Kogan
approximation~\cite{Kogan2007} for $z \gg \Lambda$):
% Kogan2007 (Pearl film field decay; monopole approximation for Delta_Bz vs. r and z)
\begin{equation}
  \Delta B_z(z) = -B_\mathrm{bias}\,\frac{\Lambda}{2z}
  = -B_\mathrm{bias}\,\frac{\lambda_L^2}{z\,d_\mathrm{SC}}.
  \label{eq:oq1_deltaB}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Design recommendation:} Use $B_\mathrm{bias} \geq 1$\,mT and NV standoff
$z \leq 10\,\mu$m for sub-second detection. For D0-Easy (bulk diamond, $z \sim 10\,\mu$m),
$B_\mathrm{bias} = 1$\,mT gives $\Delta B_z \approx 80\,\mu$T, detectable in $< 0.1$\,s.

\subsection{Critical Field (Meissner Stability)}

The applied bias field must not exceed the NbN lower critical field $H_{c1}$ to keep
the film in the Meissner state. For 200\,nm NbN~\cite{Kamlapure2010}:
% Kamlapure2010 (NbN critical field context; thin-film limit for Meissner stability)
$B_{c1} \sim 10$--$50$\,mT at 12\,K. The recommended $B_\mathrm{bias} = 0.1$--$1$\,mT
is far below $B_{c1}$, keeping the film fully screened.

\subsection{OQ-1 Status}

\textbf{Status: RESOLVED.} The Meissner--Zeeman signal is detectable with Jacob's existing
apparatus at the D0 operating point. The only remaining blocker is the NbN film itself
(Section~\ref{sec:nbn-sourcing}).
