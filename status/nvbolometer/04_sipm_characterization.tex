\section{SiPM Characterization \& Cryo Behavior}
\label{sec:sipm-cryo}

The Hamamatsu S14420-3050MG SiPM is the photon detector for the D1 readout chain.
This section summarizes its room-temperature specifications, cryogenic dark-count-rate
(DCR) behavior, and the critical breakdown-voltage temperature dependence that governs
safe operation at 12\,K.

\textbf{Note on active area.} The datasheet specifies $\varphi\,3.0$\,mm (diameter),
giving area $= \pi(1.5\,\mathrm{mm})^2 \approx 7.07$\,mm$^2$. A common approximation
treats this as $3 \times 3$\,mm$^2 = 9$\,mm$^2$; we use 7.07\,mm$^2$ for all rate
calculations. The DCR density at 25$^\circ$C is:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{DCR}_{\mathrm{density}}(298\,\mathrm{K})
  = \frac{1.6 \times 10^6\,\mathrm{cps}}{7.07\,\mathrm{mm}^2}
  \approx 226\,\mathrm{kHz}\,\mathrm{mm}^{-2}.
  \label{eq:dcr_density_rt}
\end{equation}
% S14420datasheet p.2 (DCR 1600 kcps total); active area circular phi 3mm per p.1

\subsection{DCR Temperature Model}
\label{sec:dcr-model}

\subsubsection{Physics of DCR Temperature Dependence}

Dark counts in SiPMs originate from three mechanisms~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}:
% Liu2022SiPMLN2 (DCR mechanisms review at cryogenic temperatures)

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Thermal generation} — electron-hole pairs generated by Shockley--Read--Hall
    (SRH) trap-assisted thermal excitation in the depletion region:
    \begin{equation}
      \mathrm{DCR}_{\mathrm{thermal}}(T) = C\,T^2\,\exp\!\left(-\frac{E_a}{k_B T}\right),
      \label{eq:srh}
    \end{equation}
    where $E_a$ is the effective activation energy for SRH trap-assisted generation
    (substantially lower than $E_g/2 \approx 0.56$\,eV due to mid-gap trap levels),
    $T$ is absolute temperature, and
    $k_B = 8.617 \times 10^{-5}$\,eV\,K$^{-1}$~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}.
    % Liu2022SiPMLN2 p.7 Eq.(2): I_thermal = AT² exp(-E/kBT); SRH thermal generation model
    This mechanism dominates at $T \gtrsim 100$\,K.

  \item \textbf{Tunneling current} — band-to-band tunneling and trap-assisted tunneling
    (TAT). Dominates at $T \lesssim 100$\,K; nearly temperature-independent~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}.
    % Liu2022SiPMLN2 (tunneling floor plateau below 123 K)

  \item \textbf{Afterpulse-generated DCR} — trapped carriers released after an avalanche
    re-trigger the cell. Accounted for separately in noise simulations.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Calibration Against Published Cryogenic Data}

Using a Shockley--Read--Hall (SRH) thermal generation model calibrated against published
cryogenic data for ON~Semiconductor J-Series SiPMs (literature calibration;
PDF in hand: \texttt{hardware/sipm\_fpga/references/SiPM\_cryo\_submeV\_arXiv2509.11954.pdf}),
the effective SRH activation energy is $E_a \approx 0.107$\,eV~\cite{SiPMcryoSubmeV2025}.
% SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (SiPM cryo characterization: DCR vs. T model, activation energy)
% internal: hardware/sipm_noise_characterization.tex Sec.~2 (SRH model, E_a ~ 0.107 eV)

This value ($E_a \approx 0.107$\,eV $\ll E_g/2 \approx 0.56$\,eV) reflects that the
dominant DCR generation mechanism is mid-gap SRH trap-assisted generation rather than
band-to-band thermal excitation~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}.
% Liu2022SiPMLN2 (SiPM DCR dominated by SRH trap states at sub-gap energies)

\subsubsection{DCR Extrapolation to 12\,K}

At $T = 12$\,K, the thermal SRH contribution is negligibly small (Eq.~\ref{eq:srh}
evaluated at 12\,K gives a suppression factor $\sim 10^{-43}$ relative to RT). The
dominant DCR mechanism becomes tunneling-floor current.

Using the sub-meV detector data~\cite{SiPMcryoSubmeV2025}, the tunneling floor
plateaus at $\approx 5$\,mHz\,mm$^{-2}$ at 10\,mK for ON Semi J-60035.
% SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (measured: DCR ~5 mHz/mm² at 10 mK for J-60035, V_over = 2.5V)
Since tunneling is nearly temperature-independent below 50\,K, extrapolating to
12\,K for the S14420 (7.07\,mm$^2$):
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{DCR}_{\mathrm{tunnel}}(12\,\mathrm{K})
  \approx 5\,\mathrm{mHz}\,\mathrm{mm}^{-2} \times 7.07\,\mathrm{mm}^2
  \approx 0.035\,\mathrm{cps}.
  \label{eq:dcr_12k}
\end{equation}
% SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (tunneling floor method); S14420datasheet p.1 (7.07 mm² active area)
% internal: hardware/sipm_noise_characterization.tex Sec.~2.4 (extrapolation to 12K)

The project conservative estimate of 10\,cps DCR at 12\,K is approximately 285$\times$
higher than the tunneling-floor extrapolation (Eq.~\ref{eq:dcr_12k}). This discrepancy
is plausibly explained by overvoltage dependence (higher $V_\mathrm{over}$ exponentially
increases tunneling DCR~\cite{SiPMcryoSubmeV2025}) or differences between the S14420
epitaxial structure and the J-Series devices used for calibration.
% SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (DCR vs. V_over at 10 mK: exponential rise approaching second breakdown at ~5V)

\subsection{Critical Safety Constraint: Breakdown Voltage vs.\ Temperature}
\label{sec:vbr-temp}

\textbf{This is the most important pre-D1 measurement required.}

The S14420 breakdown voltage shifts with temperature at approximately
$dV_\mathrm{BR}/dT \approx -47$\,mV/$^\circ$C~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}.
% Liu2022SiPMLN2 (SiPM VBR vs T characterization; ~47-54 mV/K typical for Si APD devices)
% S14420datasheet p.2 Table (temperature coefficient of V_op = 47 mV/degC)
% internal: hardware/sipm_vbr_temperature_compensation.tex (VBR(T) model and extrapolation)
Extrapolating from room temperature to 12\,K ($\Delta T = 263$\,K):
\begin{equation}
  V_\mathrm{BR}(12\,\text{K}) \approx 42\,\text{V} - 0.047\,\text{V/K} \times 263\,\text{K}
  \approx 28.6\,\text{V}.
  \label{eq:vbr_cryo}
\end{equation}
% S14420datasheet p.2 (V_BR typ = 42V); Liu2022SiPMLN2 (dV_BR/dT ~ 47-54 mV/K)

\subsection{Literature Survey: Comparable SiPMs at Cryogenic Temperature}

Published cryogenic SiPM characterization covers several comparable
devices~\cite{SiPMcryoArrays2025,SiPMcryoSubmeV2025,Liu2022SiPMLN2}:
% SiPMcryoArrays2025 (SiPM cryo 40--300 K, gain and noise behavior)
% SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (sub-meV detector SiPM cryo characterization)
% Liu2022SiPMLN2 (SiPM at LN2 and cryo temperatures: VBR, DCR, gain)
\begin{itemize}
  \item At 77\,K (LN$_2$): DCR drops by $3$--$4$ orders of magnitude vs.\ room temperature;
    gain increases due to lower thermal noise in avalanche region~\cite{Liu2022SiPMLN2}.
    % Liu2022SiPMLN2 (DCR vs T, LN2 data for J-Series)
  \item At $< 50$\,K: tunneling-floor DCR dominates; values in the
    0.01--100\,cps range depending on device and overvoltage~\cite{SiPMcryoSubmeV2025}.
    % SiPMcryoSubmeV2025 (sub-meV detector: DCR at 10 mK vs V_over)
  \item At 40--300\,K: SiPM array gain and correlated noise behavior characterized
    for DM detector applications~\cite{SiPMcryoArrays2025}.
    % SiPMcryoArrays2025 (array characterization 40-300K range)
  \item \textbf{The S14420 is not characterized below $-40^\circ$C in any published
    study.} Direct measurement at 12\,K is required (G-GAP-03).
\end{itemize}
