\section{Pixel Architecture \& Fabrication}
\label{sec:pixel-fab}

This section summarises the NbN bolometer pixel design constraints (OQ-7). Full derivations
are in \texttt{oq7\_pixel\_architecture.tex}.

\subsection{NbN Film Thickness and $T_c$}

The AJA ATC ORION UHV sputtering system is confirmed on-site at the UT Arlington
Shimadzu NRC (\url{https://shimadzuinstitute.org/centers/nanotechnology-research-center/}).
Reactive DC magnetron sputtering of Nb in N$_2$/Ar at $\sim\!150^\circ$C substrate temperature
achieves $T_c = 16.58$\,K at 200\,nm film thickness
(source: \url{https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10697750/}).

For D0, the design target is 200\,nm NbN on $c$-plane sapphire with $T_c \geq 15.7$\,K.
Normal-state resistivity $\rho_n \approx 150\,\mu\Omega\,\text{cm}$, used in the
Wiedemann--Franz thermal conductivity estimate, is from
Kamlapure et al.~\cite{Kamlapure2010} and Sidorova et al.~\cite{Sidorova2020}.
% Kamlapure2010 Table I (rho_n for NbN film with T_c ~ 15.87 K)
% Sidorova2020 Table III (rho_n for NbN films; Wiedemann-Franz thermal conductivity)

\subsection{Pixel Geometry and Energy Resolution Floor}

Phonon-noise-limited energy resolution (linear calorimeter approximation)~\cite{Richards1994}:
% Richards1994 Eq. (17) p.8 (E_res = sqrt(4 k_B T^2 C); phonon noise floor)
\begin{equation}
  E_\mathrm{res}^\mathrm{ph} = \sqrt{4\,k_B\,T^2\,C} \propto \sqrt{V}\,T,
  \label{eq:pixel_E_res}
\end{equation}
where $C = c_v V$ is the pixel heat capacity.
\textbf{Note:} This formula applies to a TES calorimeter, not the D0 threshold detector.
Values are retained as geometry comparison figures of merit; the physically correct D0
sensitivity metric is the threshold energy $E_\mathrm{th}$ (Section~\ref{sec:oq5}).

\subsection{Demonstrator Specifications}

\textbf{D0-Easy} (current plan): Blanket NbN film on existing diamond in existing cryostat.
$T_c$ target $\geq 15.7$\,K; NV standoff $z = 10$--$100\,\mu$m; bias field 0.1--1\,mT.
No pixel patterning required. Success criterion: repeatable ODMR frequency shift correlated
with $R(T)$ drop at $T_c$.
% Kamlapure2010 Table I (T_c ~ 15.87 K for epitaxial NbN, confirms >= 15.7 K target)

\textbf{D0-Opt} (parallel design): $1 \times 1 \times 0.20\,\mu$m patterned pixel via
contact photolithography at Shimadzu NRC. Fe-55 X-ray source (5.9\,keV) as calibration
input; signal-to-noise of $5900\,\text{eV} / 17.6\,\text{eV} = 335\times$ above the
phonon floor.

\subsection{Magnetic Crosstalk Constraint (Multi-Pixel Arrays)}

The controlling pitch constraint for multi-pixel arrays is Pearl-field magnetic crosstalk.
Pearl length for 200\,nm NbN:~\cite{Pearl1964}
% Pearl1964 p.65-66 (defines Lambda = 2 lambda_L^2 / d)
\begin{equation}
  \Lambda = \frac{2\lambda_L^2}{d} = \frac{2 \times (400\,\text{nm})^2}{200\,\text{nm}}
  = 1.6\,\mu\text{m}.
  \label{eq:pearl_length_section}
\end{equation}
using $\lambda_L = 400$\,nm for NbN at 12\,K~\cite{Kamlapure2010}.
% Kamlapure2010 (lambda_L = 400 nm from penetration depth measurement; internal: oq1)
The resulting pitch constraint is $p \gtrsim 3\,z_\mathrm{NV}$.
For single-pixel D0/D1: magnetic and thermal crosstalk are irrelevant.
% internal: oq7_pixel_architecture.tex Sec.~4-5 (crosstalk analysis, Q3-Q4)

\subsection{Architecture Evolution to P1/P2}

Temperature scaling from the D0 baseline (48\,eV at 12\,K)~\cite{Richards1994}:
% Richards1994 Eq. (17) p.8 (E_res propto T^{3/2} sqrt(V))
\begin{equation}
  E_\mathrm{res}^\mathrm{ph} \propto T^{3/2}\,\sqrt{V}.
  \label{eq:T_scaling}
\end{equation}
Going to 4.2\,K (LHe) alone reduces the D0-Easy floor from 48\,eV to 3.6\,eV
(factor $13\times$). P1/P2 targets require $T \leq 2.5$\,K (He-3 sorption cooler) and
replacement of NbN with Al ($T_c = 1.10$\,K) for the SC film.
